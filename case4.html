Study: 4/5

Serving a new audience ASAP to fight virus by streamlining platform ideation & launch

Global scientists were core users. How to adapt for a worried public?

  • A user experience needed to be quickly conceptualized, approved, and tested for a non-technical audience.
  • We had to fulfill high-profile commitment as a STEM publisher to supporting public health response to Zika.
  • Our team leveraged relationship with community to execute content strategy for target audience.

This type of project was new to us. We instantly pulled together as the situation developed.

👤

My role: Product Owner & Content Strategist responsibilities.

The team: Stakeholders from Communications and Marketing & Sales based in NYC, Heidelberg, and Berlin. Subject contributors from Americas, Europe, and Asia. Client: Springer Nature, a global publisher. €1.4B revenue.

Core business: Information platforms, technology solutions, and traditional media for professionals.

SPRINGER
NATURE

My design process was fast & practical since seconds mattered during the outbreak.

1

Step 1 | Problem:

Understanding what unique value we could add to the global effort was my priority.

  • No time to talk with users due to urgency.
  • Quickly researched activities across the industry.
  • Discovered our media capabilities & community connections.
  • Discovered an underserved audience with an information gap we could address.
2

Step 2 | Definition:

Identifying target users as undergraduates & advanced high schoolers gave my decisions focus.

  • We would share the personal experiences of scientists to users exploring career choices.
  • To add ongoing value, I balanced agility & consistent structure as the crisis developed.
  • The concept needed to engage users despite the rigid design of our corporate site.
3

Step 3 | Deliver:

From the start, I thought in terms of how to make contributing to the site as easy as possible.

  • Contributors were scientists, so I drafted content guidelines accessible to a wider audience.
  • A template with repeat components supported a consistent user experience.
  • Portuguese & Spanish (translations helped us reach populations most impacted by the crisis.

A minimally viable site was launched within 7 weeks followed by quick growth.

  • Since the user story resonated with the scientific community, we organized scientist authors.
  • We surpassed our goal of 10 contributions by 150%.
  • Visits from Brazil, Canada, Germany, and the USA, and others within 3 months according to my limited metrics.
