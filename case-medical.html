Study: Avoiding a $5M product failure by analyzing user & business needs for medical learning tool

I used design thinking to analyze a strategy change,
with the program's future at stake.

  • Executives needed to make a go/no-go decision on a $5M medical learning tool — without reliable user data.
  • The existing product direction was misaligned with what physicians and medical students actually needed.
  • A reframing of business and user needs was required before any design work could proceed responsibly.

My role

UX Researcher & Strategist

Project

Research on strategic opportunity, risks, and roadmap

Client

Multinational media company

Step 1 | Discovery

User research revealed a fundamental mismatch between product vision and physician workflow.

I conducted interviews and competitive analysis to understand how medical professionals actually learn — and how far the product was from those realities.

  • Interviewed medical students, residents, and attending physicians to map how they consume clinical knowledge.
  • Key user insight surfaced early: "Textbooks are dead!" — users had moved entirely to digital, on-demand learning.
  • Assessed three competing products to benchmark features and identify underserved user needs.
  • Produced a strategic opportunity map showing where the product could compete sustainably.

Step 2 | Flow Diagrams

I diagrammed the flow after a solutions architect once used one to review specifications with me.

Reframing the core business & user needs led to a better solution as desired.

  • Enabled client to picture how the user would move through the product if we implemented their instructions.
  • The problem I had described became real and tangible to the client through the visual flow.
  • Client became invested in a solution rather than attached to their original feature list.
  • Flow diagrams proved to be the critical communication tool that aligned engineering, design, and business stakeholders.
Strategic flow diagram — revised user journey

Revised flow diagrams showing the new recommended user journey structure

Step 3 | Deliver

Strategic recommendations gave executives the evidence they needed for a confident go decision.

I delivered a research-backed strategic roadmap that reframed the product from a direct competitor to a specialist tool for a defined, underserved audience.

  • Presented findings and flow diagrams to C-suite stakeholders; execs greenlit the redesigned direction.
  • Produced a phased product roadmap with clear success metrics tied to user archetypes.
  • Recommended a content architecture that supported the "on-demand, searchable" mental model users described.
  • Identified a six-figure expansion opportunity in the attending physician segment that the original plan had overlooked.
"Reframing the core business & user needs led to a better solution as desired."

The increased integrity of the design boosted client's $6-figure expansion plan.

  • More robust results will be achieved via efficiencies that encourage users to more frequently access the app.
  • Our outlook for future business opportunities improved thanks to positive collaboration.
  • Flow diagrams proved to be a useful communication tool equipping clients to make more satisfying decisions.
