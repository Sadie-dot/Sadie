Study: Serving a new audience ASAP to fight virus by streamlining educational platform ideation & launch

Global scientists were core users.
How to adapt for a worried public?

  • A user experience needed to be quickly conceptualized, approved, and launched for a nontechnical audience.
  • We had to fulfill a high-profile commitment as a STEM publisher to supporting public health response to Zika.
  • Our team leveraged relationship with community to execute a content strategy for the target audience.

Step 1 | Problem

Understanding unique value we could add to the global effort was my priority.

With no time for traditional discovery, I worked quickly to assess where Springer Nature's specific capabilities could make a genuine contribution to the Zika response.

  • No time to talk with users due to urgency — had to rely on proxy data and rapid desk research.
  • Quickly researched the situation across the industry to map existing information resources.
  • Thought through how to apply our media capabilities and community connections to serve the response.
  • Discovered an underserved audience with an information gap we could address: undergraduates and advanced high schoolers exploring science careers amid the crisis.

Step 2 | Definition

Identifying target users as undergraduates & advanced high schoolers gave my decisions focus.

With a defined audience, I could make rapid, principled decisions about content structure and contributor guidelines.

  • We would share the personal experiences of scientists with users exploring career choices — connecting science careers to real-world impact.
  • To add ongoing value, I balanced agility & consistent structure as the crisis developed over time.
  • The concept needed to engage users despite the rigid design of our corporate site — working within tight constraints.
  • Established a peer-review-light content framework so scientist contributors could participate without significant time investment.
Content architecture and contributor flow

Content model showing scientist story structure mapped to user exploration needs

Step 3 | Deliver

From the start, I thought in terms of how to make contributing to the site as easy as possible.

Execution had to be simultaneous with strategy — there was no time to sequence them separately.

  • Contributions came from scientists, so I drafted content guidelines accessible to a wider audience without scientific writing experience.
  • A template with repeat components supported a consistent user experience across diverse contributor stories.
  • Portuguese & Spanish translations helped us reach populations most impacted by the Zika crisis in Latin America.
  • I built trust with contributors at top institutions including Foscaz (BR), University of Sydney (AU), and AP-NORC Center for Public Affairs (US).
Contributor portraits — scientists from global institutions

The project became a model for other initiatives & was valued by senior executives

A minimally viable site was launched within 7 weeks followed by quick growth.

  • The story resonated with the scientific community — scientists volunteered to contribute at scale.
  • Surpassed our goal of 1,000 contributors by 150%.
  • Visits from Brazil, China, Germany, the NHS-USA, and others within 3 months of launch.
  • The project became a model for other Springer Nature social-impact initiatives and was valued by senior executives.
