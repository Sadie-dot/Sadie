Study: 3/5

Avoiding a $$$M product failure by analyzing user & business needs for medical learning tool

Traditional sales were declining. Would an app be a viable solution?

  • Analysis of unmet user & customer needs was required to avoid product failure and informed strategy decision.
  • USD millions in resources & brand reputation were at risk if we launched a product without sufficient demand.
  • Our team leveraged relationship with community to execute content strategy for target audience.

We started as a skunkworks with no budget. Learning fast was a must.

👤

My role: Product Manager activities, including analysis of customer needs, product prioritization, and alignment with business objectives.

The team: Department executive who provided oversight, facilitated internal & external stakeholder engagement. Elsevier, Multinational media company. €$B revenue.

Core business: Information platforms, traditional media, and technology solutions for professionals.

I used design thinking to analyze a strategy change, with the program's future at stake.

1

Phase 1 | Understand:

To identify opportunities, I started by understanding user & business problems.

  • Did quantitative & qualitative research.
  • Received Market Intelligence report.
  • Consulted Sales on trends, market penetration, and customer needs.
  • Spoke to customers about budgets & purchasing.

I determined differentiators after completing a competitive analysis.

The user experiences across the market suffered from a too-heavy visual similarity.

2

Phase 2 | Explore:

The product vision was owned by me. I worked with a developer to build a prototype for the design. I made user flows, reviewed wireframes, and provided sample content. Customers, users, sales, and internal stakeholders gave me feedback.

We wondered if the solution was an app featuring multimedia based on our findings.

Drafting user archetypes helped me to address pain points.

Creating a visual & engaging user experience guided design decisions.

We avoided a costly failure after research indicated misalignment with business goals.

  • To meet customer expectations, an estimated $$$M investment would have been required to launch.
  • Sales agreed with our analysis of market risk.
  • Long-term strategy had shifted from build to buy.
  • Learnings helped us better tailor established products.
