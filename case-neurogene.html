Study: Preserving a client relationship with proactive communication as a critical bug risked disaster

A failed launch could shutter client.
App had a fatal flaw — now what?

  • We had to maintain trust despite a showstopper bug discovered before user acceptance testing (UAT).
  • Not delivering in time would jeopardize the success of client's company, including their financial backing.
  • I liaised with our engineers & the client to keep stakeholders informed of developments until solved.

Problem

Bug @ user acceptance testing (UAT) risked patients

Project

App to record seizures & medication for caregivers

Client

Boston-based start-up
First-time client

Step 1 | Triage

Assess the severity of the bug and its impact on the launch timeline.

When the showstopper bug emerged, I immediately coordinated with engineers to understand the technical scope and estimate resolution time.

  • Worked with QA and the engineering lead to classify the bug's severity and patient-safety implications.
  • Assessed contractual obligations and whether a delayed UAT was legally and commercially viable.
  • Determined that transparency with the client was essential — concealing the issue risked greater damage to the relationship.
Bug triage documentation

Severity assessment and escalation flow used to brief the client

Step 2 | Communicate

Proactive, structured communication kept all stakeholders aligned under pressure.

I drafted a communication plan and served as the single point of contact between our engineering team and the client's stakeholders.

  • Sent a structured status update to the client immediately after triage — before they could discover the issue independently.
  • Established a daily written update cadence so stakeholders had predictable information at a set time each day.
  • Used plain language, avoiding technical jargon, so non-technical client stakeholders could relay accurate information to their investors.
  • Provided a revised realistic timeline with contingency built in rather than optimistic projections.
Sample stakeholder update email

Email template used for daily structured status reports to client

Step 3 | Resolve

Bug resolved, trust preserved, and relationship strengthened for future work.

Once engineers resolved the bug, I coordinated a re-UAT plan and debriefed with the client on lessons learned.

  • Delivered a post-incident review document outlining what happened, what we did, and how our process would prevent recurrence.
  • Client stakeholders reported that the communication during the crisis was the most reassuring part of the engagement.
  • The first-time client renewed for a follow-on engagement, citing trust built during the incident as a key factor.

Transparent communication during a crisis converted a near-disaster into a lasting client relationship.

  • UAT completed successfully after bug resolution with no patient-safety incidents.
  • Client retained for follow-on project, citing trust and honesty as primary reasons.
  • Communication framework became a team standard for high-stakes client incidents.
